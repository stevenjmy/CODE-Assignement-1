Civic Link in Parramatta CBD

Civic Link is 490m long and 20m wide, connecting from the Parramarra Square to River Square. It will support liveability by creating a pedestrain and cycle-friendly avenue through Parramatta CBD, fitting for the increased population of Parramatta. It will support sustainability by creating a green public space in order to against side effects of climate change, collect stormwater, and satisfy the infrastructure needs. It will support productivity by linking the heritages and developing new art and culture precincts. Its linkage provides opportunities to share culture, knowledge, and experiences for creative and active people in Parramatta.







The Transformation



Government is going to build an open green corridor by connecting the Parramarra Square and River Square, which looks like a conceptual bridge between the Parramatta River and the Train Rail.










Key moves



*Be an identifiable place.
*Connect The Parramatta Square and The River Square in physical and symbolic level.




*Demolish the Horwood Place Car Park and rebuilt and 70 Macquaire Street, which are critical to the Civic Link because these two site block the way.




*Recognise and respond to heritage along the Link.




*Develop adjoining commercial spaces.
*Adjust public transport in surrounding area.
